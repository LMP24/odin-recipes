Harira

Harira is a traditional Moroccan Lentil and Chickpea soup. Because it is loaded with fiber and protein it is often eaten during the month of Ramanan to break the day fast after the sun goes down. There are many many variations of Harira, often including a type of meat, lentils, chickpeas, pasta or rice in a richly spiced tomato base.

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion
  • 1/2 celery
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 can of chickpeas
  • red and green or brown lentils
  • chicken broth
  • 1 can of tomatoes
  • 100 g of rice
  • cilantro
  • parsley
  • spices: ginger, pepper, cumin, tumeric, saffron, paprika, cayenne pepper and cinnamon
  • lemon
  • honey

How to make it

Step one

Saute the onions until they look seethrough.

Step two

Add the other vegetables and the spices. Saute them for a few minutes until they develope their flavor.

Step three

Add the chicken stock, the canned tomatoes the saffron, the lentils and half of each fresh herb. Bring it all to simmer and keep it their for 30 min.

Step four

Add the rice and let it cook for 10 min. Add the chickpeas.

Step five

Serve the soup with freshly chopoped herbs and a splash of lemon juice.
